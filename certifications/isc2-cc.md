# ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC)

**Earned:** December 5, 2025  
**Exam:** ISC2 CC

---

## What This Certification Covers

### Domain 1: Security Principles
- CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
- Authentication, Authorization, and Accounting (AAA)
- Non-repudiation and privacy principles
- Risk management fundamentals

### Domain 2: Business Continuity, Disaster Recovery & Incident Response
- Business continuity planning
- Disaster recovery procedures
- Incident response lifecycle
- Change management and configuration control

### Domain 3: Access Control Concepts
- Physical and logical access controls
- Principle of least privilege
- Segregation of duties
- Access control models (DAC, MAC, RBAC)

### Domain 4: Network Security
- Network architecture and design
- Network protocols and ports
- Network attacks and countermeasures
- Secure network components

### Domain 5: Security Operations
- Data handling and classification
- Logging and monitoring
- Configuration management
- Security awareness training

## Study Approach

**Timeline:** ~3-4 weeks

**Resources Used:**
- ISC2 Official Study Guide
- ISC2 free training materials (members get access)
- Physical notecards for key concepts
- Practice questions from ISC2 resources

**My Study Cycle:**
1. Read ISC2 official study materials
2. Take notes on security principles and frameworks
3. Take practice questions to identify weak areas
4. Create notecards for terminology and concepts I struggled with
5. Review notecards until scoring 90%+ on practice questions
6. Night-before refresh of key frameworks and principles

**Key Focus:** ISC2 wants you to think like a security professional, not just memorize facts. Understand **why** controls exist, not just what they are.

## What I Learned

This certification shifted my mindset from "IT support" to "security operations":
- Risk-based thinking (every decision involves tradeoffs)
- Defense-in-depth principles
- Importance of governance and policy frameworks
- How security operations fit into business objectives

---

## Tips for Future Test-Takers

1. **Understand the "why"** - ISC2 tests decision-making, not just memorization
2. **Focus on principles over tools** - They want framework understanding, not vendor specifics
3. **Read questions carefully** - They often ask for "best" or "most appropriate" answer
4. **Use ISC2's free resources** - The official materials align directly with exam content
5. **Think like a professional** - Choose answers that balance security with business needs
