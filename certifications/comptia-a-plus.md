# CompTIA A+ Certification

**Earned:** October-November 2025  
**Exams:** Core 1 (220-1101) and Core 2 (220-1102)

---

## What This Certification Covers

### Core 1 (220-1101)
- Hardware fundamentals (motherboards, CPUs, RAM, storage)
- Networking concepts (TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, wireless)
- Mobile devices and peripherals
- Virtualization and cloud computing basics
- Hardware troubleshooting methodology

### Core 2 (220-1102)
- Operating systems (Windows, macOS, Linux)
- Security fundamentals (malware, authentication, encryption)
- Software troubleshooting
- Operational procedures and documentation
- Basic scripting and remote access

---

## Study Approach

**Timeline:** ~6-8 weeks total (Core 1 + Core 2)

**Resources Used:**
- Professor Messer video series (free on YouTube)
- Crucial Exams practice tests
- Physical notecards for targeted weak areas
- Hands-on practice with hardware when possible

**My Study Cycle (Per Exam):**
1. **Watch Professor Messer videos** - Take notes on key concepts
2. **Take practice test** - Identify knowledge gaps
3. **Create targeted notecards** - Only for topics I missed or struggled with
4. **Study notecards** - Focus on weak areas until consistent
5. **Retake practice tests** - Repeat until scoring 90%+
6. **Night-before refresh** - Review notecards one final time

**Key Principle:** Practice tests guide what to study, notecards fill the gaps. No wasted effort on topics already mastered.

## What I Learned

This certification provided foundational IT knowledge that's critical for cybersecurity work:
- Understanding how systems operate at the hardware and OS level
- Troubleshooting methodology applicable to security investigations
- Networking fundamentals required for understanding traffic analysis
- Baseline knowledge for enterprise environments

---

## Tips for Future Test-Takers

1. **Don't skip Core 1** - The networking section is essential for security roles
2. **Memorize port numbers** - They show up repeatedly (80, 443, 22, 3389, etc.)
3. **Understand troubleshooting steps** - CompTIA wants you to follow their methodology
4. **Practice exams are critical** - Crucial Exams format mimics real test scenarios
5. **Create notecards for everything** - Physical writing aids retention

## Relevance to Detection Engineering

While A+ is an entry-level IT cert, the knowledge directly applies to detection work:
- Understanding process creation and services (Windows Event ID 4688)
- Networking fundamentals for analyzing suspicious traffic
- OS-level visibility into what "normal" system behavior looks like
